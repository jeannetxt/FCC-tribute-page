Audrey Hepburn

A timeline of Audrey Hepburn's Life and Legacy:

  • 1929: Audrey Hepburn is born in Brussels, Belgium, on May 4.
  • 939-1945: Experiences the hardships of World War II and the Dutch famine during the German occupation, which shapes her character.
  • 1948: Moves to London to pursue a career in ballet and modeling.
  • 1951: Makes her acting debut in the European film "One Wild Oat."
  • 1953: Achieves international stardom with her role in "Roman Holiday," which garners her an Academy Award for Best Actress.
  • 1954: Continues to establish herself as a Hollywood icon with her performances in "Sabrina" and "War and Peace."
  • 1957: Stars in "Funny Face" and "Love in the Afternoon," showcasing her versatile acting skills.
  • 1961: Portrays the iconic character Holly Golightly in "Breakfast at Tiffany's," which further cements her status as a fashion and film legend.
  • 1963: Appears in "Charade" alongside Cary Grant, receiving critical acclaim for her performance.
  • 1964: Wins her second Academy Award for her role as Eliza Doolittle in "My Fair Lady."
  • 1967: Marries Italian psychiatrist Andrea Dotti, with whom she has a son, Luca Dotti.
  • 1969: Divorces Mel Ferrer and marries Andrea Dotti.
  • 1988: Begins her humanitarian work as a Goodwill Ambassador for UNICEF, traveling to some of the world's most impoverished regions.
  • 1992: Publishes her memoir, "Audrey Hepburn: An Elegant Spirit," offering insights into her life and experiences.
  • 1993: Receives the Presidential Medal of Freedom, the highest civilian award in the United States, in recognition of her dedication to humanitarian causes.
  • 1993: Audrey Hepburn passes away on January 20 in Tolochenaz, Switzerland, after a battle with appendix cancer.
  • 2002: Her legacy continues through the Audrey Hepburn Children's Fund, established to honor her memory and further her mission of improving the lives of children around the world.

Audrey Hepburn's life was marked by her extraordinary contributions to film and her unwavering commitment to humanitarian work. Her legacy endures as she remains an enduring symbol of elegance, grace, and compassion.
